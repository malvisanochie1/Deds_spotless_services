Tank Cleaning

Cleaning of storage tanks, silos, and reactors to remove residues, sediments, or build-up. Techniques include manual cleaning, spray ball cleaning, and robotic cleaning.

Read more
  • Remove residues, sediments, and build-up
  • Manual interior tank scrubbing
  • Spray ball cleaning system usage
  • Robotic tank cleaning for large units
  • Safe disposal of waste materials

Steam Cleaning

Uses high-temperature steam to clean surfaces, equipment, and machinery. Effective for removing grease, grime, and bacteria without harsh chemicals.

Read more
  • Apply high-temperature steam on surfaces
  • Remove grease and grime from equipment
  • Sanitize without using harsh chemicals
  • Eco-friendly disinfection of sensitive areas
  • Dry and polish cleaned surfaces

Pressure Washing

High-pressure water jets used to clean surfaces such as floors, walls, and equipment. Suitable for removing dirt, oil, and paint.

Read more
  • Use high-pressure water jets on surfaces
  • Clean industrial floors, walls, and exteriors
  • Remove oil stains and paint layers
  • Suitable for concrete, tiles, and metals
  • Ensure drainage and drying after wash

Chemical Cleaning

Use of specialized chemical solutions to remove stubborn residues, rust, or scale. Includes acid cleaning, alkaline cleaning, and solvent cleaning.

Read more
  • Apply acid or alkaline cleaning agents
  • Remove rust, scale, and stubborn build-up
  • Use appropriate PPE and chemical handling
  • Flush and neutralize treated surfaces
  • Dispose of chemical waste safely

Electrostatic Cleaning

Uses electrostatically charged particles to coat and clean surfaces evenly. Common in electronics and sensitive equipment cleaning.

Read more
  • Spray charged cleaning particles
  • Ensure even surface coverage
  • Target electronics and sensitive equipment
  • Reduce cross-contamination risk
  • Quick drying with no residue

Vacuum and Dry Ice Cleaning

Uses industrial vacuums or dry ice pellets to remove dust, debris, or residues without water or chemicals. Dry ice cleaning is environmentally friendly and non-abrasive.

Read more
  • Use industrial vacuum for dry waste removal
  • Apply dry ice pellets on target surfaces
  • Non-abrasive removal of grime and residues
  • Safe for electrical or delicate machinery
  • No water or chemical residues left behind
